## 1. Domain overview

The business is an intermediary between financial advisers (servicing high-net-worth clients) and banks that issue structured investment products. It operates in AUD and USD and manages the full lifecycle of products: structuring, client subscriptions (“trades”), funding, settlement, ongoing coupons, maturities, and reconciliation.

## 2. Core entities

### 2.1 Advisors & client accounts
- Advisors create and manage multiple client accounts in the platform.
- Client accounts are used to subscribe into products.

### 2.2 Products
- Each product has:
  - **Internal Product Name** (used operationally and in reporting)
  - **ISIN** (used by issuing banks and market infrastructure)
  - Other metadata such as issue date, currency, and total target size.
- A product is funded via one or more client trades.

### 2.3 Trades
- A **trade** = one client account subscribing an amount into a specific product.
- Key fields:
  - Client account
  - Trade amount (AUD or USD)
  - System-generated **Trade Number** (e.g. `T00001`, `T16121`).
- One product can have many trades; one advisor can submit many trades into the same product.

## 3. Funding flow

- Clients (or advisors) transfer money to the company’s bank account to fund trades.
- **Primary identifier for funding:** Trade Number, typically referenced as `"T" + number` (e.g. `T16121`) in the bank transaction description.
- Missing or incorrect trade references create exceptions that require manual matching.

### 3.1 Product funding status

For each product, the system tracks:
- Expected trades (by Trade Number and amount)
- Total product target amount

As deposits are matched to trade numbers:
- The count of funded trades increases.
- The total received amount for the product increases.

A product is considered **fully funded** when all trades are funded and the product’s target amount is received.

## 4. Settlement

Once a product is fully funded, operations “settle” it by:

1. Sending principal to a **settlement account** (e.g. Clearstream).
2. Sending revenue (fees/margin) to a **revenue account**.

Typical bank transaction patterns (referencing product name):
- Principal transfer: description like `"MS 2026-02-1"`
- Revenue transfer: description like `"MS 2026-02-1 - REV"`

After these movements, the initial purchase lifecycle for that product is complete.

## 5. Ongoing cashflows: distributions & maturities

### 5.1 Distributions (coupons)
- Periodic payments from issuing banks.
- Identified and referenced primarily by **ISIN**, not by internal product name.
- Often received as a **single aggregated credit** (e.g. “CPNs”) that corresponds to multiple products/ISINs.
- Operations use issuer portals to:
  - Break down the aggregate amount into ISIN-level lines.
  - Record these breakdown lines internally.
  - Later allocate/payout these amounts to client accounts.

### 5.2 Maturities (principal repayments)
- Return of principal at scheduled maturity or in case of early termination.
- Also identified primarily by **ISIN**.
- May arrive as single or aggregated payments and must be split internally by ISIN, then allocated to products and client accounts.
